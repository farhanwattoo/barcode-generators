Before you start: choose the right barcode type

The single most important decision when creating a barcode is choosing the correct symbology. Using the wrong barcode type — for example, a QR code on a product meant for retail POS scanning — will result in a barcode that cannot be read by the intended system.

How to create a retail barcode (UPC-A or EAN-13)

Retail barcodes are more involved than other types because the product number you encode must be globally unique and officially registered.

Step 1 — Get a GS1 company prefix

To create a legitimate UPC-A or EAN-13 barcode for retail sale, you must first obtain a GS1 company prefix from GS1 — the non-profit organisation that administers barcode numbers globally. GS1 charges an annual fee for company prefixes. Visit gs1.org or your country's GS1 member organisation to register. Do not purchase barcodes from third-party resellers who sell individual GTINs — major retailers check GS1 databases and can reject products whose GTINs are not registered to the brand.

Step 2 — Assign a GTIN to each product

Once you have your prefix, assign a unique item reference number to each distinct product (each size, colour, and variant needs its own number). Combine your company prefix with the item reference to form a GTIN-12 (for UPC-A) or GTIN-13 (for EAN-13).

Step 3 — Generate the barcode image

Enter your GTIN into the UPC-A barcode generator or EAN-13 barcode generator. The tool will calculate the check digit and render the barcode. Download as SVG for packaging artwork files.

Step 4 — Check minimum size requirements

A UPC-A barcode has a nominal size of 37.29mm x 25.91mm at 100% magnification. GS1 specifications allow a range from 80% to 200% of this size. Printing smaller than 80% risks scan failures at POS. Always verify the final printed barcode meets GS1 print quality requirements.

How to create a shipping barcode (Code 128)

Shipping barcodes are simpler than retail barcodes because you do not need to register your numbers with any authority — you define the data yourself.

  1. Decide what data to encode: order number, tracking number, serial number, or a combination using GS1 Application Identifiers (if shipping to a major retailer)
  2. Open the Code 128 barcode generator
  3. Enter your alphanumeric data. Code 128 handles letters, numbers, and symbols
  4. Download as SVG or high-resolution PNG
  5. Place on your shipping label alongside the human-readable text version
Shipping to major retailers? If you ship to Walmart, Target, Amazon, or similar large retailers, they likely require GS1-128 format (not plain Code 128) with specific Application Identifiers encoding your SSCC, GTIN, batch number, and expiry date. Check your retailer's routing guide before creating labels.

How to create a QR code

QR codes are the simplest barcode type to create because any text, URL, or data is valid — no registration or special numbering is required.

What your QR code can encode

  • URL — links to a website, landing page, or product page
  • Plain text — any message or information up to ~4,000 characters
  • Email — opens a pre-addressed email draft when scanned
  • SMS — opens a pre-addressed text message
  • Phone number — initiates a call when scanned
  • WiFi credentials — automatically connects the scanner's device to a WiFi network
  • vCard — saves a contact card to the scanner's address book

Error correction levels

QR codes have four error correction levels: L (7% tolerance), M (15%, a good default), Q (25%, good for packaging), and H (30%, required if you add a logo inside the QR code). Higher levels make the code more damage-tolerant but reduce data capacity.

Minimum QR code size

A QR code must be at least 2cm x 2cm (approximately 0.8 inches) when printed for reliable smartphone scanning. For billboard or large-format printing, scale proportionally based on the scanning distance.

How to create a barcode in Excel

There are two ways to add barcodes to an Excel spreadsheet. The barcode font method works for Code 39 — install a free Code 39 font, enter your data in a cell with asterisks around it (="*"&A1&"*"), and apply the barcode font to that cell. For EAN-13 and UPC-A barcodes, the more reliable approach is to generate the barcode using the online tool, download as PNG, and insert it as an image via Insert → Pictures.

How to create a barcode in Word

The same two approaches apply in Microsoft Word: the barcode font method for simple Code 39 labels, or generating the barcode externally and inserting as an image via Insert → Picture. When resizing in Word, always maintain the aspect ratio — never stretch a barcode horizontally as this changes bar widths and makes it unscannable.

Printing and size requirements

A correctly generated barcode can still fail to scan if printed incorrectly. Follow these rules to ensure your printed barcodes are always scannable:

Resolution

Print at a minimum of 300 DPI. For very small barcodes or barcodes with narrow bars (like Code 128 at high density), use 600 DPI or higher. Always use SVG or PDF source files for print, not JPEG which introduces compression artefacts that blur bar edges.

Colours

Bars must be dark and the background must be light. Avoid red bars (wrong wavelength for laser scanners), light-coloured bars, and dark backgrounds. Black bars on white is the most reliable combination.

Quiet zones

Every barcode requires blank space — called a quiet zone — on each side. For UPC-A and EAN-13, this is at least 2.31mm on each side at nominal size. Never allow packaging artwork, text, or graphics to encroach into the quiet zone. This is one of the most common causes of barcode scan failure on retail packaging.

Frequently asked questions

How do I create a barcode for free?
Use a free online barcode generator. Choose your barcode type, enter your data, click Generate, and download as PNG, SVG, or PDF. No software or account needed.
What data do I put in a barcode?
For UPC/EAN retail barcodes, encode your product's registered GTIN. For Code 128 shipping barcodes, encode your order or tracking number. For QR codes, encode a URL, contact card, WiFi details, or any text.
Can I create a barcode for my product to sell in stores?
Yes — but for retail barcodes (UPC-A or EAN-13) you must first register a GS1 company prefix to obtain a legally recognised GTIN. Once you have a valid GTIN, generate the barcode image free using the online tool.
What file format should I use for my barcode?
SVG for professional print (scales to any size without pixelation). PNG for digital use and documents at 300 DPI. PDF for direct printing. Never use JPEG.
How do I test if my barcode is correct?
Scan the generated barcode with a smartphone camera and confirm the decoded value matches your intended data. For retail barcodes, also verify the GTIN is registered in the GS1 database.
